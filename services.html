Candlestick charts display price action over time, helping traders identify trends, reversals, and market sentiment. Each candlestick shows the open, high, low, and close prices for a given period. Mastering chart reading is key to making informed trading decisions.

Forex Pairs

In Forex, currencies are traded in pairs (e.g., EUR/USD). The first currency is the base, and the second is the quote. If EUR/USD = 1.10, it means 1 Euro equals 1.10 US Dollars. Pairs are classified into majors, minors, and exotics based on liquidity and usage.

CFDs (Contracts for Difference)

A CFD is a financial contract that allows traders to speculate on the price movement of an asset without owning it. In Forex, you don‚Äôt buy actual currencies‚Äîinstead, you trade the price difference between entry and exit points. Profits (or losses) are based on how much the market moves in your favor (or against you).

What is a Broker?

A Forex broker is a platform that provides traders access to the foreign exchange market. They act as intermediaries, executing buy and sell orders on your behalf. Brokers offer trading platforms, tools, leverage, and access to various currency pairs and CFDs. Choosing a reliable broker is key to a safe and efficient trading experience.

Types of Brokers

There are two main types of Forex brokers: Dealing Desk (Market Makers) and No Dealing Desk (ECN/STP). Market Makers create their own prices and often take the opposite side of a client‚Äôs trade. ECN/STP brokers connect traders directly to liquidity providers, offering tighter spreads and more transparency.

Forex Regulators

Forex regulators are government or independent agencies that ensure brokers operate fairly and transparently. Reputable regulators include FCA (UK), ASIC (Australia), CySEC (Cyprus), and FSCA (South Africa). Always verify if your broker is regulated‚Äîit protects your funds and ensures accountability.
